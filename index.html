Sequence-to-sequence Voice Conversion-based Techniques for Electrolaryngeal Speech Enhancement in Noisy and Reverberant Conditions

Authors: Ding Ma, Yeonjong Choi, Takuya Fujimura, Fengji Li, Chao Xie, Kazuhiro Kobayashi, Tomoki Toda
Comments: Submitted to APSIPA Transactions on Signal and Information Processing.
Abstract: Electrolaryngeal (EL) speech is artificial speech produced using an exectrolarynx to aid laryngectomees communicate without vocal fold vibrations. Compared to normal speech, EL speech lacks essential phonetic features and has a different temporal structure, resulting in poor naturalness, speaker identity, and intelligibility. In the context of addressing these deficiencies, sequence-to-sequence (seq2seq) voice conversion (VC) emerges as a promising technique owing to the suitability of its mechanism for the challenges in EL-speech-to-normal-speech conversion (EL2SP). Nonetheless, state-of-the-art VC studies for EL2SP mostly focus on converting clean EL speech and overlook further applicability in real-world communication scenarios where EL speech is inevitably interfered with background noise and reverberation. To enhance the robustness of EL2SP in such conditions, we suggest a novel and efficient seq2seq VC-based training method. In contrast to the extended VC that relies on the extra augmentation modules to tackle interferences, our method requires the single framework of seq2seq VC. First, we pretrain a normal-to-normal seq2seq VC model, adapted from a text-to-speech model. Then, we employ a two-stage fine-tuning in a many-to-one style, effectively leveraging the pseudo noisy and reverberant EL speech data generated from only a small amount of clean data available. We investigated several system designs to figure out the effectiveness of our method. The intermediate representations of these systems were also analyzed to understand their role in filtering interferences. The comparative experiments demonstrate that our method significantly outperforms EL2SP baselines, non-trivially handling both clean EL speech and noisy-reverberant EL speech, which sheds light on the possible improving directions.

Main concept

EL2SP dataset

Source EL data, which is an EL corpus recorded from a laryngectomee; Target normal data, which is a nomral courpus recorded from a healthy speaker.

Two experimental conditions for the evaluation

  • Clean condition: Convert the clean EL speech to clean target normal speech.
  • Noisy-Reverberant condition: Convert the noisy-reverberant (NR) EL Speech to clean target normal speech.

Eleven types of speech were evaluated.

  • Referenced speech:
    • Source: Clean EL speech (C-EL), NR EL speech (NR-EL).
    • Target: Clean normal speech (C-SP).
  • Converted speech from Baseline 1:
    • Conversion in clean condition: C-B.1
    • Conversion in NR condition: NR-B.1
  • Converted speech from Model 1:
    • Conversion in clean condition: C-M.1
    • Conversion in NR condition: NR-M.1
  • Converted speech from Model 2:
    • Conversion in clean condition: C-M.2
    • Conversion in NR condition: NR-M.2
  • Converted speech from Model 3:
    • Conversion in clean condition: C-M.3
    • Conversion in NR condition: NR-M.3
